    Career and Professional Life

    Moving on to her career, she made her Broadway debut as Maureen Johnson in the rock musical Rent, for which she received a Tony Award nomination for Best Featured Actress in a Musical.

    Moreover, after appearing in several smaller-scale stages and Off-Broadway productions, she was cast as Elphaba in the Broadway musical Wicked in 2003. The musical’s success, as well as her character and song “Defying Gravity,” earned her a devoted following among theater fans.

    However, after leaving the stage show in 2005, she returned to the original West End production becoming the highest-paid actress in West End theatre history. She returned to Broadway in 2014 as Elizabeth Vaughan for which she was nominated for a third Tony Award.

    Furthermore, in the early 2000s, she began to play in film and television roles. She was cast as Nancy Tremaine in Disney’s musical fantasy film Enchanted in 2007. Similarly, she appeared as Shelby Corcoran on the musical television series Glee from 2010 to 2013.

    Also, she has been the voice of Elsa in Disney’s animated Frozen franchise since 2013. “Let It Go,” a song she recorded for the first film, became a huge hit upon its release, winning an Academy Award for Best Original Song and peaking at number five on the Billboard Hot 100. In addition, she also appeared in the 2019 crime drama Uncut Gems and the jukebox musical Cinderella.
